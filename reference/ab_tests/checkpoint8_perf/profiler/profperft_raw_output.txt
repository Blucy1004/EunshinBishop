=== Interpretation ===
Position::doMove accounts for ~70-72% of total perft wall time (both depths
agree closely, confirming the measurement is stable). Within doMove, the
three explicitly-instrumented sub-phases (isPseudoLegal re-validation,
king-attacked legality check, checkers/attackersTo computation) together
account for only ~31.4% of doMove's own time (~36.9% at depth 5,
~36.9% at depth 6) -- none of them individually dominate. The remaining
"rest of doMove" bucket (hashing, board mutation, bookkeeping -- i.e.
everything between the StateInfo setup and the final checkers assignment)
is ~62.5-63.1% of doMove's time on its own, roughly double any single
instrumented sub-phase and larger than all three combined.

This pointed the investigation at doMove's StateInfo setup rather than at
isPseudoLegal (the initial hypothesis going into this profiling run).
Reading src/position/position.cpp's doMove and src/position/state.h /
src/core/nnue_state.h together showed the concrete cause: doMove opened
with `newState = StateInfo{};`, a full zero-initialization of the struct
-- including its embedded NNUE::AccumulatorState, which holds
std::array<std::array<int32_t, 256>, 2> (2048 bytes). Every field of
StateInfo is then explicitly assigned an real value later in the same
function (the accumulator field itself is overwritten two statements later
via `newState.accumulator = oldState->accumulator;`, another full
2048-byte write). The only field not explicitly (re)assigned before the
zero-init's effect could matter was `checkers`, which is unconditionally
assigned near the end of doMove before any successful return, and is never
read on a failed-move path (callers already discard StateInfo when doMove
returns false, per the existing perft/generateLegalMoves/isLegal contract).

Net effect: every doMove call was performing two full-size writes to the
2048-byte accumulator array (one from the redundant zero-init, one from the
real copy) where one was necessary. At perft(6)'s 125,049,325 doMove calls,
that is roughly 250 MB of entirely avoidable memory traffic on top of the
250 MB of necessary accumulator-copy traffic -- consistent with "rest of
doMove" being memory-copy-bound and dominating over the three
computation-bound sub-phases.

The fix (applied to the real project, not this throwaway worktree) removes
the redundant `newState = StateInfo{};` and instead explicitly assigns
`newState.checkers = EMPTY_BB;` alongside the block's other explicit field
assignments, at negligible cost (one 8-byte write) versus zero cost of
correctness risk (every field is still explicitly assigned before use).
See src/position/position.cpp's Position::doMove and the code comment
added there. Debug-config CTest (which exercises `assert(isConsistent())`
after every doMove/undoMove) passed after the fix, and perft(6) remained
exactly 119,060,324 on the real project's Release build.

This worktree (C:\profwt) and its instrumentation are diagnostic-only and
are not part of the shipped project; they are discarded after this
checkpoint. This file and tools_profperft.cpp are preserved here purely as
evidence of the profiling method and raw results.
