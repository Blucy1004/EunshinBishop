Even with NNUE fully disabled, Q's search throughput is still
substantially lower than the reference's, consistent with
perft6_speed_comparison.txt's finding that the gap is rooted in
move-generation/make-unmake throughput, not NNUE inference cost -- NNUE
mode widens the reference/Q NPS ratio further (2.6-2.8x with NNUE vs
3.8-4x classical-only reported here is the OPPOSITE direction from what
"NNUE inference cost dominates" would predict, since Q's own NNUE-mode gap
is actually smaller than its classical-mode gap here; treat this specific
comparison as a single-position data point, not a general throughput
model, and re-derive from a larger sample before relying on it further).
