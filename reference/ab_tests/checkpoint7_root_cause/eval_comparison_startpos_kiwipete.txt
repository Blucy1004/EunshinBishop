*** SIGN FLIP ***: Classical (103 vs 103) and NNUE raw (-131 vs -131) again
match exactly between engines. The reference's blend keeps the FINAL score
positive (+42 cp, agreeing with classical's own verdict that the position
favors the side to move) because NNUE is only 35% of the combination. Q's
residual-additive formula adds the full -131 cp on top of +103 cp classical,
overturning the sign entirely: Q evaluates this exact position as -28 cp
(favoring the OPPONENT), a 234 cp swing from the classical-only assessment
and the opposite conclusion from the reference.

ResidualGuard did not intervene (factor 100%) because its knee threshold
(400 cp at this position's phase) is well above this 131 cp correction --
by design (`src/eval/evaluator.cpp`'s `applyResidualGuard`: "Preserve
ordinary tactical corrections exactly. Only the excess above a 4-pawn
endgame / 6-pawn opening knee is compressed"). This is not a guard bug; the
guard is doing exactly what it was written to do. The finding is that a
131 cp "residual" that reverses a 103 cp classical verdict does not look
like the kind of "small correction" the residual-additive design assumes,
which raises the question (not yet answered / NOT investigated further per
instruction to stop before changing code) of whether FIRST_NET v5's raw
output is actually calibrated as `teacher - classical`, or is closer to an
independent, comparably-scaled absolute evaluator for which the reference's
blend approach is inherently more robust than a 100%-weight addition.
